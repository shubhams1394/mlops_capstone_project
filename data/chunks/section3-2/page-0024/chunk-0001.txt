Agricultural Loans 
 
Introduction 
 
Agricultural loans are an important component of many 
community bank loan portfolios.   Agricultural banks 
represent a material segment of commercial banks and 
constitute an important portion of the group of banks over 
which the FDIC has the primary F ederal supervisory 
responsibility. 
 
Agricultural loans are used to fund the production of 
crops, fruits, vegetables, and livestock, or to fund the 
purchase or refinance of capital assets such as farmland, 
machinery and equipment, breeder livestock, and farm real
