The examiner should ensure the bank has performed a title 
and lien search of the property prior to taking a mortgage 
or advancing funds.  Proper procedure calls for an 
abstractor bringing the abstract up to date, and review of 
the abstract by an attorney or title insurance company.  If 
an attorney performs the task, the abstract will be 
examined and an opinion prepared indicating with whom 
title rests, along with any defects and encumbrances 
disclosed by the abstract.  Like an abstractor, an attorney is 
liable only for damages caused by negligence.  If a title 
insurance company performs the task of reviewing the
